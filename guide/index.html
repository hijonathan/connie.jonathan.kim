Hawaii Travel Guide

Things to do while in Hawaii

While you're on Oahu, we recommend extending your stay to enjoy enjoy more of what the island has to offer. There are also a few other great islands to visit, depending on what you're looking for.

Oahu — North Shore (1-2 days)

The North Shore of Oahu, known for it's surf beaches, see it's biggest swells in the winter. October will be mild, but you'll find pockets of surfers everywhere.

  • Stop by Greenworld Coffee Farm on the drive up to sip coffee among coffee trees
  • Check out the pineapple fields at Dole Plantation
  • Visit Waimea beach and jump off the big rock
  • Check out the local farmer's market in Waimea Valley, which sells fresh fruit, local jams/honey, nuts and local plate lunches (Wednesday only)
  • Cruise up toward Pipeline, one of the most famous surf spots in the US
  • Swim with the sharks
  • Grab shave ice and a burger in Haleiwa town

Windward (1-2 days)

The west side of Oahu offers some of the most beautiful seaside scenery and hikes on the entire island.

  • Eat lilikoi pancakes at Moke's
  • Hike Kuliouou, Lanikai Pillboxes, Diamond Head, Maunawili Falls and Makapu'u Lighthouse trail
  • Visit Kualoa Ranch, where they filmed Jurassic Park
  • Scope out Kaneohe Bay

Honolulu (1-2 days)

While most of downtown Honolulu is designed for tourists, there are several great spots that cater to locals and visitors who appreciate the island culture.

Food

  • Eat fresh poke/fish from Maguro Brothers
  • Enjoy hot malasadas from Leonard's Bakery
  • Pick up late-night cream puffs from Liliha Bakery
  • Grab plate lunch at Alicia's Market
  • Dine on huge slabs of sushi from Sushi Murayama
  • Put your spice tolerance to the test with Agu Ramen
  • Don't forget to pick up spam musubi from Zippy's (or anywhere, really)
  • Walk through dozens of Japanese food stalls at Shirokiya in Ala Moana
  • At night, stop by Lucky Belly for a drink and tips on the nightlife

Museums

  • Honolulu Museum of Art
  • Honolulu Museum of Art Spalding House
  • Iolani Palace
  • Arizona Memorial (it's actually pretty cool and cheap)

Outer Islands

Each island offers something a little different, but the three most commonly visited are:

Big Island. Probably not a good idea right now given the volcanic activity, but the Big Island strikes a nice balance of being tourist-friendly without losing the local charm.

Maui. The second biggest island and known for it's beaches, Maui is beautiful but very touristy.

Kauai. If you're looking to get off the grid for a while, go to Kauai.
